0 to F Hexadecimal Counter - Febuary 2024

Building this 0 to F counter was one of my proudest moments. It pushed me to understand logic gates, IC datasheets, and how they all come together in a working digital system.

↓

The Main Circuit

This is the main circuit I built and tested using Logisim. It uses a 4-bit counter made from JK flip-flops with a timer. Connected to a decoder that sends the right signals to a 7-segment display, letting it count from 0 to F in hexadecimal.

The Decoder

This is what it looks inside the decoder from the main circuit. Now this is the most complicated part, since I only have access with 7448 7-Segment decoder, which is only 0 to 9. I had to improvise with the actual logic of the A to F. This is the whole hexadecimal decoder which took almost two of my breadboards.

The Protyping Process

Putting theory into action! I tested the decoder first since it was the trickiest part. I hooked it up to 4 buttons acting as the 4-bit input, along with LEDs to see if the signals were flowing right. It took the most time because I had to make sure every gate worked just like in the simulator.
